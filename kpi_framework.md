# KPI Framework — Amman Digital Market

Define 5 KPIs for the Amman Digital Market. At least 2 must be time-based and 1 must be cohort-based.

---

## KPI 1

- **Name:** Monthly Revenue
- **Definition:** The total revenue generated from valid customer purchases in each month.
- **Formula:** Sum of `quantity × unit_price` grouped by `order_month`
- **Data Source (tables/columns):**
  - `orders.order_date`
  - `order_items.quantity`
  - `products.unit_price`
  - after excluding `orders.status = cancelled`
  - after excluding `order_items.quantity > 100`
- **Baseline Value:** Total cleaned revenue across the analysis period was **48,701.50**
- **Interpretation:** This KPI shows the overall sales trend over time and helps the business identify stronger and weaker sales months.

---

## KPI 2

- **Name:** Monthly Order Volume
- **Definition:** The number of valid orders placed in each month.
- **Formula:** Count of unique `order_id` grouped by `order_month`
- **Data Source (tables/columns):**
  - `orders.order_id`
  - `orders.order_date`
  - `orders.status`
- **Baseline Value:** The cleaned dataset contains **443 valid orders**
- **Interpretation:** This KPI helps track customer demand over time and can be compared with monthly revenue to see whether growth comes from more orders or larger order values.

---

## KPI 3

- **Name:** Revenue by City
- **Definition:** The total revenue generated by customers in each city.
- **Formula:** Sum of `quantity × unit_price` grouped by `city`
- **Data Source (tables/columns):**
  - `customers.city`
  - `customers.customer_id`
  - `orders.customer_id`
  - `order_items.quantity`
  - `products.unit_price`
- **Baseline Value:** **Amman** was the top revenue-generating city in the cleaned dataset.
- **Interpretation:** This KPI shows which customer locations contribute the most revenue and can help managers decide where to focus promotions or customer retention efforts.

---

## KPI 4

- **Name:** Average Order Value by Product Category
- **Definition:** The average category-level order contribution across product categories.
- **Formula:** Mean of category-level order revenue grouped by `category`, where category-level order revenue = sum of `quantity × unit_price` for each `order_id` and `category`
- **Data Source (tables/columns):**
  - `products.category`
  - `products.unit_price`
  - `order_items.quantity`
  - `order_items.order_id`
- **Baseline Value:** **Books** had the highest average order value at about **70**
- **Interpretation:** This KPI helps identify which categories tend to produce higher-value purchases and which categories may deserve more prominent placement or stronger promotion.

**Statistical Validation**
- **H₀:** Average order value is the same across all product categories.
- **H₁:** At least one product category has a different average order value.
- **Test Used:** One-way ANOVA
- **Why Appropriate:** The comparison is between the average values of more than two independent groups.
- **Test Statistic:** **56.7853**
- **p-value:** **2.1069868717112423e-52**
- **Effect Size:** **Eta squared = 0.2111**
- **Interpretation:** Reject H₀. Average order value differs significantly across categories, and the effect size suggests a meaningful practical difference.

---

## KPI 5

- **Name:** Revenue by Registration Cohort
- **Definition:** The total revenue generated by customers grouped by their registration month.
- **Formula:** Sum of `quantity × unit_price` grouped by `registration_month`
- **Data Source (tables/columns):**
  - `customers.registration_date`
  - `customers.customer_id`
  - `orders.customer_id`
  - `order_items.quantity`
  - `products.unit_price`
- **Baseline Value:** Revenue contribution differed across registration cohorts, showing that some customer signup periods contributed more value than others.
- **Interpretation:** This cohort KPI helps the business evaluate whether customers acquired in certain registration periods were more valuable over time.

---

## Additional Statistical Test

### Revenue Difference Between Top Two Cities
- **Related KPI:** Revenue by City
- **H₀:** There is no significant difference in customer revenue between the top two cities.
- **H₁:** There is a significant difference in customer revenue between the top two cities.
- **Test Used:** Independent samples t-test
- **Groups Compared:** **Amman** and **Irbid**
- **Test Statistic:** **-0.1201**
- **p-value:** **0.9053**
- **Effect Size:** **Cohen's d = -0.0378**
- **Interpretation:** Fail to reject H₀. Although Amman generated the most total revenue, the per-customer revenue difference between the top two cities was not statistically significant in this sample.

### City and Product Category Association
- **Related KPI:** Revenue by City / purchasing behavior by city
- **H₀:** Customer city and product category are independent.
- **H₁:** Customer city and product category are associated.
- **Test Used:** Chi-square test of independence
- **Test Statistic:** **20.6990**
- **p-value:** **0.8971**
- **Effect Size:** **Cramer's V = 0.0619**
- **Interpretation:** Fail to reject H₀. The dataset does not show a meaningful association between customer city and product category.
